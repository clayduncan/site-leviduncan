# UI Brand Handoff

## Purpose

This handoff captures the preferred Clay Duncan website visual direction for future UI strategy and implementation work. It is especially focused on the banner/background assets in `public/images/` and how they should influence the site palette, hero treatment, typography, and overall feel.

## Approved Direction

Clay likes the tone, theme, and style of the existing banner background and wants to keep that direction as a core visual reference for the personal site.

At minimum, the Clay hero should preserve the image treatment where Clay appears in front of the warm modern interior background. The site may also use the banner/background style elsewhere, but it should be adapted into a polished website system rather than copied as a repeated wallpaper.

The approved service-page visual direction is **Concept A · Warm Modern Authority**, first tested at `/concepts/va-a/` and applied to `/huntsville/va-loans/`. Future landing pages should build from this direction unless a page-specific reason calls for a deliberate variation.

Primary visual reference assets:

- `public/images/Clay-Duncan-wbackground.jpg`
- `public/images/google-Clay-Duncan.jpg`
- `public/images/image-background.jpg`
- `public/images/linkedin-Clay-Duncan-banner.jpg`
- `public/images/youtube-Clay-Duncan-banner.jpg`

## Strategic Design Position

The site should feel like a personal authority website, not a Princeton Mortgage clone site.

Best description:

**Warm modern mortgage authority.**

The design should say:

- Clay is experienced and trustworthy.
- The mortgage conversation is serious but approachable.
- The site is personal, polished, and high-quality.
- Princeton Mortgage is clearly present as the lending affiliation, but not the full visual identity.

Avoid making the site feel like:

- A generic mortgage template.
- A corporate Princeton microsite.
- A dark social-media banner stretched into a whole website.
- A luxury real estate magazine with weak mortgage clarity.

## What To Preserve From The Banner Style

The strongest cues from the visual assets:

- Bright natural light.
- Warm wood tones.
- Black metal and window framing.
- Soft white and off-white surfaces.
- Pale blue from Clay's shirt.
- Glass and open space.
- Greenery as a small human/local accent.
- Upscale home interior mood.
- Calm, structured, confident composition.

These cues should influence the full visual system.

## Hero Guidance

Clay's hero should use the Clay portrait with the warm modern interior background as the primary first-impression asset unless a future photoshoot creates a stronger real-life equivalent.

Hero goals:

- Clay should be visually present in the first viewport.
- The background should support the "home, clarity, confidence" feeling.
- Text should remain highly readable.
- The hero should feel warm and premium, not dark or gimmicky.
- The layout should work cleanly on mobile without awkward cropping of Clay's face or text overlap.

Implementation notes:

- Use the Clay-with-background asset as the hero image or create a responsive crop system from it.
- Consider separate desktop and mobile crops if needed.
- Keep copy and CTAs on a clean readable surface or in an area of the image with enough contrast.
- Avoid placing long text directly over visually busy wood/window areas unless a subtle overlay is used.
- Do not repeat the full hero image on every page.
- For service landing pages, use an asymmetric editorial hero: strong left-side copy, real/brand-relevant image on the right, concise callout overlay, and proof strip near the hero.
- Hero callouts should use warm/cognac accenting rather than bright blue or lime.

## Palette Direction

Keep the palette direction from the banner and Clay's personal brand, with Princeton-compatible accents.

Approved palette family:

- Warm off-white: `#f4f1ea`
- Deep charcoal / soft black: `#141414`
- Trust navy: `#24305f`
- Action blue: `#2c6ef6`, used mainly for links and selected interactive emphasis
- Primary CTA navy: `#24305f`
- Warm wood / cognac accent: `#b17845`
- Dark cognac hover/accent: `#8a522c`
- Muted sage/olive accent: `#697845`
- Soft sage surface: `#eef1e5`
- Princeton lime: use rarely and only as a tiny energetic accent

Use Princeton colors as affiliation and trust accents, not as the entire site identity.

The site should be:

- Personal-brand first.
- Princeton-compatible.
- Warm, bright, confident, and readable.

It should not be:

- Navy/blue/lime everywhere.
- Beige-heavy or brown-heavy.
- A one-note warm palette.
- A dark gray banner theme across the whole website.
- Bright-blue CTA heavy when navy would feel calmer and more premium.
- Princeton-lime heavy in process sections, cards, or major UI elements.

## CTA Color Rules

- Primary CTAs should use deep navy fill with white text.
- Primary CTA hover should shift to dark cognac.
- Secondary CTAs should use an outlined navy treatment.
- Secondary CTA hover should shift border/text to dark cognac.
- Bright blue should remain available for links or special action emphasis, but it should not dominate the page.

## Typography Direction

Typography should feel polished and clear.

Approved direction:

- Source Serif 4 for strong, refined editorial headings.
- Inter for highly readable body copy and UI text.
- Clean sans-serif UI text for navigation, buttons, labels, proof strips, metadata, cards, and small utility copy.
- Editorial influence is welcome, but mortgage clarity comes first.

Do not let the site become overly fancy, fragile, or luxury-magazine-like. The typography should support trust and fast comprehension.

## Image Usage Rules

Use the background image as a brand atmosphere asset, not as the whole brand.

Recommended usage:

- Primary Clay homepage hero.
- Selective section backgrounds or crops.
- Social/profile visual consistency.
- A subtle visual reference for premium mortgage and homeownership themes.

Use real Clay/community/training/local photos for authenticity across the rest of the site where possible.

Avoid:

- Repeating the same interior background on every page.
- Letting the background overpower Clay or the offer.
- Using stock-like interior imagery where the visitor needs local or human trust.
- Using the dark angled social banner layout as the main website design language.

## Service Landing Page Visual Pattern

Use the VA Loans page as the first approved visual baseline:

1. Warm off-white editorial page base.
2. Strong Source Serif 4 headline.
3. Inter body/UI copy for clarity.
4. Clay or relevant real imagery in an asymmetric hero.
5. Compact hero callout with cognac accent.
6. Proof strip with warm cognac top rule.
7. Direct-answer section immediately after the hero/proof area.
8. White cards on warm neutral backgrounds.
9. Navy process or emphasis band with cognac detail.
10. Bright blue minimized so it does not overpower the personal-brand warmth.

## Princeton Mortgage Relationship

The personal site does not need to match Princeton Mortgage's color tones exactly.

The right relationship is:

**Clay brand first, Princeton affiliation clearly present.**

Princeton should be represented through:

- Company affiliation.
- NMLS/compliance language.
- Application link.
- Footer and verification details.
- Selective navy/blue/lime accent usage.
- Logo usage only where appropriate and compliant.

Princeton should not control:

- The entire palette.
- The whole hero style.
- The emotional tone of the site.
- Clay's personal authority presentation.

## Summary For UI Strategy Chat

Use the current Clay banner/background as the visual north star for the personal site. Keep Clay's hero image with the warm modern interior background at minimum. Build a warm, bright, premium, personal mortgage-authority design system around Concept A · Warm Modern Authority. Use Source Serif 4 + Inter, deep navy CTAs, cognac/warm wood as the main accent, sage as the quiet support color, and Princeton colors sparingly as affiliation and trust accents. The result should feel like Clay Duncan's personal authority site: polished, human, home-focused, credible, and easy for borrowers to understand quickly.
