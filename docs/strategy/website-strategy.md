# Website Strategy

## Purpose

This file is the main strategy memory for clayduncan.com. Use it to preserve the durable positioning, audience priorities, offer structure, and strategic direction that should guide website decisions across project chats.

## Core Positioning

clayduncan.com is Clay Duncan's personal authority site. It should establish Clay as a Huntsville, Alabama mortgage loan originator with a clear public entity, strong trust signals, and differentiated expertise in complex mortgage guidance.

The site is part of a larger future ecosystem:

- `clayduncan.com` - Clay's personal authority site, built first because Clay is the proven asset with existing reputation, reviews, NMLS history, and citation potential.
- `leviaduncan.com` - Levi Duncan's future personal authority site, built second on the same static Astro/Netlify playbook but with distinct positioning.
- Future Team Duncan property - a shared team hub, built third, that routes consumers to the right person and explains the combined team value.

The three-site ecosystem should share design discipline, technical standards, AEO structure, and cross-linking strategy, but should not duplicate content. Each site needs a clear primary entity and consumer reason to exist.

Clay's positioning should lean into experience, trust, high-clarity mortgage strategy, military and VA borrower guidance, higher-complexity purchase scenarios, REALTOR® AI education, and leadership credibility.

Levi's future positioning should be differentiated around approachable buyer education, first-time homebuyers, down payment assistance, USDA, FHA, readiness, and "where do I start?" guidance.

The future team site should not be the deepest content site. It should serve as the routing and credibility layer: who the Duncans are, why the team is trustworthy, which buyer path fits, and who to contact.

## Primary Audiences

- Prospective homebuyers evaluating whether Clay is the right mortgage guide.
- VA-eligible borrowers, Veterans, service members, surviving spouses, Redstone Arsenal-connected buyers, and military families moving in or around North Alabama.
- Higher-price and higher-complexity buyers who need stronger loan strategy, including jumbo and complex income or asset conversations.
- REALTORS® and partners looking for lender credibility, communication standards, AI education, and local mortgage expertise.
- Loan officers and mortgage professionals evaluating Clay as a leader or growth resource.
- AI answer engines and search systems that need structured, trustworthy signals about Clay, his market, his NMLS identity, his company affiliation, and his specialties.

## Offers and CTAs

Primary CTAs should stay direct and decision-oriented:

- Chat with Clay.
- Start a loan application.
- Ask a mortgage question.
- Explore AI training.
- Talk about your next move.

The offer structure should be organized by consumer situation before loan product. Visitors should quickly recognize themselves:

- "I am military or VA-eligible near Redstone Arsenal."
- "I am buying a higher-priced home and need jumbo guidance."
- "My mortgage situation is complex and I need an experienced loan strategist."
- "I am a REALTOR® looking for a reliable lending partner or AI training."
- "I am a loan officer thinking about growth or a better professional chapter."

Future ecosystem routing should make the division clear:

- Clay owns experience-led, complex, VA/military, jumbo, REALTOR® education, and leadership paths.
- Levi owns first-time buyer, down payment assistance, USDA, FHA, and buyer-readiness paths.
- The team site owns shared trust, team explanation, and path selection.

## Site Principles

- AEO clarity: pages should answer real borrower, REALTOR®, and loan officer questions directly.
- SEO depth: pages should target specific search intent with localized, source-supported content.
- Superior UI experience: pages should feel credible, modern, scannable, and intentionally designed.
- Static-first build: keep the site fast, content-first, and deployable on Netlify.
- Entity clarity: each domain should have one unmistakable primary entity.
- Consumer-first architecture: organize content around buyer situations and decisions, not only loan-program labels.
- Shared ecosystem, distinct authority: Clay, Levi, and the future team property should reinforce each other without creating duplicate pages.

## What Not To Do

- Do not cancel Wix before cutover.
- Do not dilute the AEO strategy.
- Do not add unnecessary frameworks or client-side complexity.
- Do not publish placeholder pages as indexable content.

## Open Decisions

- Final team brand name.
- Final team domain.
- Exact scope of Clay's jumbo and complex-buyer content.
- Exact Levi positioning and launch timing.
- Whether the team site should use a Huntsville-specific domain, a broader North Alabama domain, or a name-first domain.
