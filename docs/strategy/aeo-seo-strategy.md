# AEO and SEO Strategy

## Purpose

This file stores search and answer-engine strategy for clayduncan.com, including page architecture, target queries, schema rules, citation strategy, and source standards.

## Strategic Position

clayduncan.com should be the expert-answer site for Clay Duncan, not a broad mortgage encyclopedia. Its SEO and AEO strategy should make Clay the clearest, most trustworthy answer for serious mortgage guidance in Huntsville, North Alabama, and Redstone Arsenal-connected searches.

The strongest strategic question for the site to answer is:

> Who is Clay Duncan, and why should someone in Huntsville or North Alabama trust him with a serious mortgage decision?

Every major SEO page, AEO block, schema object, FAQ, source panel, review module, and off-site profile should support that answer.

## AEO Principles

- Lead important pages with a direct answer that can be extracted by AI answer engines.
- Keep entity signals clear: Clay Duncan, Mortgage Loan Originator, NMLS #118739, Huntsville, Alabama, Princeton Mortgage.
- Use structured headings, FAQs, source links, and schema-ready content blocks.
- Prefer official or authoritative sources for claims about loan programs, eligibility, limits, fees, and local programs.
- Use consistent entity descriptions across the site, `llms.txt`, schema, social profiles, reviews, and off-site profiles.
- Make the page's audience, offer, proof, service area, and next step visible near the top of each important page.
- Write for answer extraction: short direct answers first, then deeper context, local nuance, proof, FAQs, and source links.
- Avoid duplicating the same loan-program content across Clay, Levi, and the future team site. Use cross-links to signal who owns which depth.
- Treat FAQs as citation targets, not afterthoughts.
- Favor passages that answer real borrower, REALTOR®, and AI-answer-engine prompts in plain language.

## AEO Page Pattern

Use the `/huntsville/va-loans/` page as the baseline pattern for important SEO/AEO pages.

Each major page should include:

- A direct answer near the top.
- A clear statement of who the page is for.
- Local relevance for Huntsville, Madison, Redstone Arsenal, or North Alabama where applicable.
- Clay's specific point of view or decision framework.
- Proof and credibility signals.
- Visible FAQs with concise, direct answers.
- Official sources where regulated or program-specific claims appear.
- A clear next step.
- Schema-ready sections that can support `FAQPage`, `BreadcrumbList`, `Service`, `LoanOrCredit`, `Person`, `LocalBusiness`, or other appropriate JSON-LD.

The ideal structure is: direct answer, audience, local context, decision factors, proof, next steps, FAQs, sources, final CTA.

## Schema Standards

- Use `Person`, `LocalBusiness`, and `WebSite` on core Clay pages.
- Use `BreadcrumbList` on indexable interior pages.
- Use `FAQPage` where the visible page includes real FAQs.
- Use `Article` once blog templates and real posts ship.
- Use `Service` or `LoanOrCredit`-style schema where appropriate for mortgage product and service pages, composed from canonical site data.
- Keep schema entity IDs stable, especially `#person`, `#business`, and `#website`.
- Keep schema data sourced from `src/data/site.ts`, `src/data/compliance.ts`, and page-level constants rather than scattering identity details throughout templates.
- Future Levi and team sites should use their own primary entity IDs and avoid accidentally marking Clay as the primary entity on Levi-owned pages.
- Keep structured data aligned with visible page content and avoid schema claims that are not supported on the page.

## Source Standards

- Use official sources for loan-program rules, eligibility, fees, limits, and compliance-sensitive claims.
- Preferred source types include VA.gov, HUD/FHA, USDA Rural Development, Alabama Housing Finance Authority where relevant, NMLS Consumer Access, Redstone Arsenal official pages, county or municipal pages, and Princeton Mortgage compliance-approved resources.
- Use local context from authoritative local sources when explaining Huntsville, Madison, Madison County, Redstone Arsenal, and North Alabama.
- Avoid unsupported rate, payment, approval, or eligibility claims.
- Treat AEO citations as an ecosystem project: website pages, Google Business Profile, YouTube, Facebook, review platforms, NMLS, industry directories, and marketplace profiles should all reinforce the same entity facts.

## Cannibalization Rules

- Do not publish duplicate deep pages across Clay, Levi, and the future team site.
- Do not make Clay the primary entity on Levi-owned pages or team-owned pages.
- Do not make Levi-likely beginner topics deeper on Clay's site than Clay-owned specialty topics.
- Do not let the future team site become a duplicate content warehouse for Clay and Levi pages.
- Use cross-links and concise routing copy to clarify ownership when topics overlap.
- Prefer one canonical owner per topic, with supporting pages linking to that owner.
